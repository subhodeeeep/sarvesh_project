About NSS

The National Service Scheme (NSS) aims at developing a sense of social responsibility, discipline, and leadership skills among students.

National Service Scheme (NSS) was introduced in 1969 with the primary objective of developing the personality and character of the student youth through voluntary communit

The ideological orientation of the NSS is inspired by the ideals of Mahatma Gandhi.

Very appropriately, the motto of NSS is “NOT ME,BUT YOU”.

An NSS volunteer places the ‘community’ before ‘self'.

  1. It is a Central Sector Scheme of the Government of India, under the Ministry of Youth Affairs & Sports.
  2. It was introduced in 1969 with the primary objective of developing the personality and character of student youth through voluntary community service2.
  3. The motto of NSS is "NOT ME, BUT YOU", reflecting its focus on community service and social responsibility.

    4. Key Objectives of NSS:

    • Understanding the Community: NSS volunteers learn about the community they work in and understand their role within it.
    • Identifying Community Needs: Volunteers identify and address the needs and problems of the community.
    • Practical Problem Solving: Utilizing knowledge to find practical solutions to individual and community problems.
    • Emergency Response: Developing the capacity to meet emergencies and natural disasters.
    • National Integration: Promoting national integration and social harmony.


      • Activities and Programs:

      • National Integration Camp (NIC): Organized annually, these camps aim to make volunteers aware of India's cultural diversity and national pride.
      • Adventure Programs: Conducted in regions like the Himalayas and Arunachal Pradesh, these programs include activities like trekking, water rafting, and para-sailing.
      • Community Service: Volunteers engage in various community service activities and programs led by the government.
      • Since its inception, NSS has spread to over 657 universities and 51 state councils/directorates, covering 20,669 colleges/technical institutions and 11,988 senior secondary schools. Over 7.4 crore students have benefited from NSS3.
